Deploy your own click-tally site

This sets up your own copy: a GitHub App in your GitHub account and a Cloudflare Worker in your Cloudflare account. Nothing is created in anyone else's account — each step runs under your logins.

Step 1 — Create your GitHub App

Creates the App under whichever account you're signed into on GitHub. You'll confirm on GitHub, then land back here with your credentials.

Advanced (optional)

Step 2 — Your App credentials

Shown once. Copy these now — this page stores nothing and can't show them again. (You can regenerate the key/secret later in your App's settings if needed.)

Step 3 — Deploy the Worker to your Cloudflare account

Click below, sign into your Cloudflare, and when prompted paste the values above. You'll also set these variables:

  • GITHUB_APP_ID, GITHUB_CLIENT_ID — from above
  • REPO_OWNER — your GitHub username
  • REPO_NAME — e.g. YOUR-USERNAME.github.io
  • SITE_URL, ALLOWED_ORIGINS — your site URL; ALLOWED_LOGINS — your username

Deploy to Cloudflare

Step 4 — Finish

  1. After deploy, copy your Worker's URL and set your App's Callback URL to <worker-url>/auth/callback (App settings → General).
  2. Install the App on your repo: Install.
  3. Point your site's front-end at your Worker URL and you're live.
